About me

I am a first-year PhD student in the Theoretical Computer Science group at the University of Birmingham, under the supervision of Jamie Vicary. My research interests are higher category theory and type theory (especially homotopy type theory). I am currently working on homotopy-io, a graphical proof assistant for higher categories based on the theory of associative n-categories.

Before this, I completed the undergraduate Master’s degree in Mathematics and Computer Science at the University of Oxford in 2015–2019. During this time, I primarily focused on category theory, topology, and theoretical computer science. For my final year dissertation, I constructed a model of Martin-Löf dependent type theory in Baues’ fibration categories, based on the work of Awodey and Warren with model categories.

Outside academia, I also have experience as an Android developer. I have developed several Android apps and I have also interned at Google twice: first in summer 2018 in the Android TV performance team in Zürich, then again in summer 2019 in the Android UI toolkit team in London (where I worked on Jetpack Compose).
